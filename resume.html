Alejandro Mosquera López - Résumé


Biography

Born and raised during the eighties in Northwest Spain, Alejandro's interest for cyber security started from an early age when seveal computers in his school lab become infected by V-sign. This fortuitous encounter attracted his curiosity towards low level programming and anti-malware research. As an undergraduate, Alejandro was an electrical engineering student, however he eventually switched to computer science and software engineering.

As a software engineer, Alejandro worked for a few years in the automotive industry, which gave him a solid foundation in enterprise IT.

As a researcher, he helped Spain's National Cybersecurity Institute CERT to map the presence of malware in P2P networks, contributed to securing mobile networks by improving the disruption of messaging threats and developed novel systems and methods to detect and prevent targeted attacks in endpoint systems. During his Ph.D., Alejandro tackled the difficult problem of making social media texts more accessible for users with functional diversity. His research led to patents, several publications and conference communications.

Alejandro Mosquera is currently a Computer Scientist and online safety expert at Broadcom Inc. where he defends large enterprises from advanced threats that evade existing security solutions.
